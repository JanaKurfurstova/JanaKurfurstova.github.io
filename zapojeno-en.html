Portal Knihovny.cz and the project itself was created thanks to the co-operation of twenty county libraries, specialized libraries and city libraries, and other libraries participate as partners. The ambition of Knihovny.cz is to connect the majority of Czech libraries in the near future.

Connected resources

  • The Union Catalogue of the Czech Republic - an electronic database which contains a list of collections held by participating libraries and other public institutions of the Czech Republic. Currently holds more than 6.2 million records of monographs, special documents and serials (journals, anthologies, proceedings, etc..) of foreign and domestic origin. Unlike portal Knihovny.cz it informs about the existence and location of particular document but does not allow to order or reserve. The Union Catalogue may not contain complete catalogs of connected libraries because of the high demands on the quality of taken records.
  • A bibliography of articles from Czech serial publications ANL - an electronics database contains a bibliography of articles from Czech magazines, anthologies and newspapers since 1990. There is more than 1,5 million records with a yearly growth of 58, 000.
  • National authorities of Czech Republic include files of personnel, corporate, topical, geographic and formal authorities to use for subject and bibliographic description.
  • Open Library - internet book database. For entries in Knihovny.cz’s it offers to borrow a digital version of publications after the registration.
  • Manuscriptorium - a freely accessible digital library which enables ready access to concentrated information on historical resources via sophisticated search tools. The objective of the project is to provide access to existing digital content via integrated tools in order to make it as readily accessible as possible.
  • Bibliographia medica Čechoslovaca - national registering bibliography, which records within the field of medicine (biomedicine) and health service all scientific publications from year 1947 published in Czech Republic (and until year 1996 also in Slovakia) as well as works of Czech authors published outside the Czech republic.
  • Standards (ČSN) - includes valid and out of date standards, which have been processed in electronic form. Full texts are available after the registration and paying the fee set by a pricelist of the Czech Office of Standards, Metrology and Testing..
  • Patents - the database of patents and utility models encompassing applications of inventions registered from year 1991, patents granted from no.1, European patents valid in Czech Republic, and registered utility models. Full texts are freely available. Joining this into portal is currently in progress.
  • Zákony pro lidi (Laws for People) - up-to-date version of Regulations of Collection of Law of the Czech Republic
  • Osobnosti regionu (Regional Notabilities) - information about significant persons from various regions. Records are enriched by mutual relations, photos and links to other additional information.
  • Pedagogical Bibliographic Database - contains annotated bibliographic records from Czech and foreign specialized pedagogical and comeniological journals and proceedings.
  • Bibliological bibliography - contains monographic studies and journal articles on the history of books, book printing and libraries in Bohemia and Moravia up to the mid-19th century, and the bibliographical work of Czech researchers without any territorial delimitation of the topic.
  • KTD: Czech Explanatory Terminology Database of Library and Information Science (TDKIV) - database of librarianship and information science managed by Library Institute NK ČR was founded in 2001-2002. It contains about 3200 complete entries (ie about 4900 terms including equivalents) representing all the essential areas of librarianship and information science and related disciplines.

Central Index = International Resources

The Central Index (accessible through the "International Resources" tab) is a large database with millions of records that contains metadata for documents from a variety of sources: ie journals published by leading world publishers of scientific literature, specialized databases, and freely available information resources.

Access to full texts that are not freely available requires registration in some of the libraries with prepaid subscribtion to the resource. However, the Central Index also provides a number of free resources available to unregistered users.

Founding members of the portal

Organizational matters of the portal are currently secured by SDRUK (Czech Association of Libraries) – together with 22 libraries it is a founding member of the portal. The libraries intensely participate in preparation of the whole portal. They delegate experts into groups which are solving partial tasks concerning the realization of the portal. These experts also rise functional comments regarding individual phases of project development, as well as they continuously integrate their stocks into the central database of the portal.

Nowadays, all libraries listed as participating libraries belong among the founding members of the portal. The rest of the founding members are among libraries in preparation to join, marked as (f).
