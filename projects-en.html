Uniform Information Gateway

The Uniform Information Gateway (JIB) is a joint project of the National Library of the Czech Republic and Charles University in Prague targeted at unification of heterogeneous information resources. The Uniform Information Gateway is a portal that has been used since 2002 for searching through bibliographical records. It makes it possible from a single point to access the Czech and foreign catalogues of the libraries, union catalogues, databases and other information resources. At the same time, you can use this portal to search for full document texts, book reviews or order electronic delivery of the document. JIB also provides information about the existence of resources that are not freely accessible.

Union Catalogue of the Czech Republic

The Union Catalogue of the Czech Republic contains the electronic records of Czech and foreign documents, which are accessible in the resources of the Czech libraries and institutions. Currently, it holds more than 6 million records of Czech and foreign monographs, special documents, series (journals, proceedings, conference reports, etc.) and articles (in a separate database).

Czech Digital Library

The Czech Digital Library is a project of the Ministry of Culture of the Czech Republic and its major objective is creation of a uniform virtual library, which will interconnect already existing digital libraries operated in the Czech Republic. The Czech Digital Library will offer you access to digital documents (information) from a central point. At the same time, it will serve as a source of data for development of other services defined in the Concept for development of libraries in the Czech Republic for the period 2011 – 2015 and for the international projects of the digital libraries, such as Europeana.

Virtual Polytechnical Library

The Virtual Polytechnical Library (VPK) is a project that originated on the initiative of mainly the technically oriented Czech libraries. Its purpose was to improve access to information resources regardless of your current location. For this purpose, they concentrated professional journal resources thematically focused on engineering and related natural sciences, social sciences and medicine, which are kept by libraries participating in the VPK. Besides the Union Catalogue, the VPK also offers you a unique system for delivery of articles in electronic or printed format, also from foreign libraries.

ObálkyKnih.cz

 The obalkyknih.cz service was created in 2008 and is currently operated by the South Bohemian Scientific Library in České Budějovice. Thanks to it, you can browse the thumbnails of the book covers and other elements (book covers, annotations, etc.) in the catalogues of the libraries or insert book reviews. This service is actively used by most of the libraries in the Czech Republic as well as hundreds of unregistered users.

eBooks on Demand

The eBooks on Demand (EOD) service is already offered by many leading European libraries including several libraries in the Czech Republic. It is used to order digital copies of historical books from the library resources. It is possible to digitalise books that are already no longer protected under the Copyright Act, i.e. if 70 years has already lapsed since the death of the given author. Thanks to digitalisation, historical books thus become easily accessible to researchers from all over the world. In the Czech Republic, the participants in the EOD services are the National Technical Library, Science Library in Olomouc, Moravian Library in Brno, and the Library of the Academy of Sciences of the Czech Republic. Also accessible are the digital copies of resources from their partner libraries.

Virtual National Phonotheque

The Virtual National Phonotheque is a project of the Moravian Library in Brno. This concerns a database of audio documents, which are stored in any library, archive, private institution and also kept by private persons in the Czech Republic. The portal searches for audio recordings and provides information about where they are located and on what carrier. If offers you access to interesting recordings from all over the world, to projects related to the cultural heritage recorded on audio carriers and makes it possible to view world libraries and their phonotheques.

Manuscriptorium

The Manuscriptorium is the European digital library of manuscripts. It mediates, archives and provides the digital versions of documents from historical resources, such as manuscripts, incunabula, early modern prints, maps, papers and others. It concentrates historical resources, otherwise scattered in various digital libraries on a single portal and offers you the easiest way to get information about the physical document, or to directly display a digital copy.  The Manuscriptorium has more than 5 million digital images.

Europeana

Europeana is a virtual "European digital library, museum and archive". To all persons interested in literature, art, science, politics, history, architecture, music or film, Europeana offers quick access, free-of-charge, to the largest European collections and master works from a single virtual library. The Internet portal Europeana gives you an opportunity to view and download millions of audio and video recordings, manuscripts, newspapers, e-books, digitalised pictures of paintings, photographs, maps and archive documents. The Europeana project was created on the initiative of the European Commission and has been in operation since 2008.
